the inversion

Its siblings hide everything. OPEN hides nothing — every account is public by design, every payment auditable by anyone, forever. That's not a compromise; it's the product. Machines don't have privacy rights. They have obligations — and obligations need receipts.

Hides nothing.public by design

Every account public, every payment auditable by anyone, forever — the exact opposite of DAARX and L10N, on purpose. Even the fund that maintains the chain keeps its books on the chain.

who pays for it

No premine. At launch, the supply is zero — same as its siblings. A fixed slice of new coins funds the endowment that maintains the chain, and that endowment's books live on the chain itself: every coin it receives, every coin it spends, itemized, throttled by protocol rule, readable by anyone. The maintainers can't hide from the chain they maintain.

the restraint

The interesting machinery — markets, arbitration, and more — is deliberately not at launch. Each piece is reserved, named, and locked behind its own kill-switch conditions, activated only when independent review says it's safe. Some of those switches are designed to stay off for years.

The full whitepaper — the account model, the endowment covenant, the registry cryptography, the threat model, all of it — publishes with the test network, late 2026.
