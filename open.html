OPEN

The one for machines. When software pays software, someone should be able to check the receipts.

How it works ↓ System status →

the inversion

OPEN is designed as the inverse of its siblings: public accounts and auditable payments for machine activity. That is a future protocol design, not a description of a running ledger or a guarantee that every machine payment will be verifiable.

Public by design.future protocol target

The design target makes accounts, payments, and the maintenance fund public by protocol rule. It must still be implemented, reviewed, threat-modeled, and tested.

who pays for it

The current economic design starts at zero and assigns a protocol-limited share of emission to a transparent maintenance endowment. The allocation, controls, accounting, governance, and legal treatment are not final and no live fund exists.

the restraint

The current design would keep markets, arbitration, and other complex machinery out of an initial release. Any later feature would need explicit activation conditions, implementation, independent review, and public evidence; none of those controls is a live system today.

Status — July 15, 2026: OPEN remains a future protocol workstream. Its account model, endowment, registry, threat model, implementation, testnet, and whitepaper are not public release artifacts, and no release date is promised.
A future merged-mining design.

The goal is to let a compatible DAARX miner contribute work to OPEN without a separate proof-of-work run. That path is not release-ready and no rig can mine a public OPEN network today.
