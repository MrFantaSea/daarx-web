PROTOCOL 03 / PUBLIC MACHINE ACCOUNTS

Receipts for machines.

A future public-accounting protocol design for machine activity: accounts, payments, and maintenance funding meant to be inspectable by rule.

OPEN is not a running ledger, mineable network, or available product. It remains a future protocol workstream with no release date promised.

01 / SYSTEM BRIEF

Privacy rule, inverted.

Private value for people. Public accounting for machines. OPEN explores that opposite trust boundary.

01

THE INVERSION

Public by protocol target.

OPEN is designed as the inverse of its siblings: public accounts and auditable payments for machine activity. That is a future protocol design, not a description of a running ledger or a guarantee that every machine payment will be verifiable.

02

THE RECEIPT

Activity meant to be checked.

The design target makes accounts, payments, and the maintenance fund public by protocol rule. It must still be implemented, reviewed, threat-modeled, and tested.

03

THE RESTRAINT

Keep machinery out at first.

The current design would keep markets, arbitration, and other complex machinery out of an initial release. Any later feature would need explicit activation conditions, implementation, independent review, and public evidence.

02 / ACCOUNTABILITY INSPECTION

Account. Receipt. Control.

These layers describe the intended boundary. They are not a product demo, live fund display, or guarantee of machine accountability.

OPN::RECEIPT_STACKSTATIC DESIGN INSPECTION

LAYER 01 / PUBLIC ACCOUNT MODEL

Attributed by design target.

OPEN explores public accounts for machine activity so payments can be inspected, attributed, and reconciled. The account model is not a public release artifact and remains incomplete.

A public account does not automatically prove that the machine or actor behind it behaved correctly.

LAYER 02 / PUBLIC PAYMENT RECORD

The ledger should show its work.

The target makes accounts, payments, and maintenance funding public by protocol rule. OPEN is not a running ledger, and there is no guarantee today that any machine payment can be verified through it.

The implementation, registry, threat model, testnet, and final whitepaper are not public release artifacts.

LAYER 03 / ENDOWMENT + GOVERNANCE

Transparent does not mean settled.

The current economic design starts at zero and assigns a protocol-limited share of emission to a transparent maintenance endowment. The allocation, controls, accounting, governance, and legal treatment are not final, and no live fund exists.

Markets, arbitration, and complex machinery remain outside the intended initial release.

03 / SPECIFICATION + STATE

A design, not a ledger.

OPEN is materially earlier than DAARX and L10N. Its provisional parameters describe a workstream, not a launch-ready network.
