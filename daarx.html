DAARX

The one to keep. A private store of value with a supply that never grows.

the supply

There will only ever be π × φ of it — about 5.083 septillion base units, chosen once, reached exactly by a hundred-year schedule, and then zero forever. No tail, no committee, no exceptions. The emission table is a frozen list of integers that anyone can recompute.

the privacy

Most privacy coins hide you in a crowd of sixteen. DAARX hides you in the whole chain — every coin ever created is part of your crowd, every time you spend. No decoys to pick badly, no ring to analyze. Amounts are hidden. The only thing the chain ever learns about a coin is if someone tries to spend it twice.

Even the fees are designed not to fingerprint you: every fee snaps to one of a short public ladder of amounts, so yours looks like everyone's.

the limits

The privacy layer is new cryptography built on an audited foundation. It is tested, not proven — so before launch, people are paid from a public bounty to break it — and every attempt gets published. If anything real is found, the launch waits. A public test network runs for months first.

The full whitepaper — every parameter, every proof obligation, every limitation stated plainly — publishes with the test network, late 2026. What ships will match it exactly; that's why it waits.
