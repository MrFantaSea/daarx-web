the questions

Is this an investment? Will I make money?

This site does not sell coins, solicit an investment in a protocol, or promise a return. Any future testnet coins would have no monetary value; if a network launches, mining could cost more than it produces and there is no guaranteed income. A hardware-interest submission is not a purchase or investment. Legal classification depends on the actual facts and jurisdiction, so qualified counsel must review the final launch and commercial terms. Nothing here is financial or legal advice.

Are the coins for sale?

No coins are sold here. The current launch design has no premine, presale, or token sale, but no public network or final launch artifact exists.

Can I mine now?

No supported public testnet miner is available. The current standalone miner is intentionally limited to regtest until its live node integration is complete. Do not buy hardware based on an expectation of first-block mining.

Is the software really free?

Development source is available, and the project intends to publish free node, miner, wallet, and release-image artifacts. Supported public release packages, signatures, update instructions, and compatibility guarantees are not available yet.

When does it ship? When can I mine?

There is no store, accepted order, shipping date, or supported public miner release. March 16, 2027 is a non-binding protocol planning target, not a hardware or delivery promise.

What's the catch?

This is unfinished, high-risk protocol and product work. Privacy can fail, software can contain defects, schedules can slip, mining can lose money, hardware estimates can change, and the network may never launch. Nothing here promises profit or availability.

Can I buy or reserve a rig?

No order or priority reservation is accepted. You may record non-binding interest, which creates no inventory hold, quote, price, priority, financing application, or delivery promise.

Is there a node-only option?

A roughly $299 Node Box is a planning concept, not a current offer. Final specifications, price, power, noise, software, support, and availability are unverified.

What happens to my hardware-interest information?

Desiderata Labs LLC receives the details you choose to submit; Web3Forms relays the submission and Proton receives the message. We use it to manage and follow up on non-binding interest, do not sell it, and handle access or deletion requests under the privacy notice.

Still have a question? Email a human at daarxmoney@proton.me.
Want to follow development?

Read the current status and, if useful, submit non-binding hardware interest. There is no public mining release, hardware order, priority reservation, or launch guarantee.
