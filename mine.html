Mine it.

When the network goes live, anyone can help secure it — on a plain computer, no warehouse. Here's how you'll take part.

The chains are mined with RandomX — the kind of mining a normal CPU does well and a datacenter doesn't. Run a node, point a miner at it, and you help hold the network up. Mining DAARX secures all three at once. The node and the miner are free and open source — so the software to do this is, and always will be, free.

read this before anything else

Participation, not profit. This is a fair-launch coin: at the start it is worth about nothing, on purpose. Early on, the power your rig draws may cost more than what it mines. You do this to own in early and help secure the network — never for income. No promise of profit, earnings, or return. If that's not why you're here, this isn't for you.

The coins are never for sale. No premine, no presale, no token sale — mining is the only way in, for everyone, and that never changes. The rig is a separate thing: it's hardware and help, sold apart from the coins. Buying a box is buying a computer, not buying coin. We keep those two completely apart, on purpose.

how hands-on do you want to be?

Same recipe, three amounts of effort — your call. The cheapest is free; the others exist only so you don't have to build one yourself.

a convenience-and-support premium over raw parts, same idea as any pre-built computer.

optional, if you want it

Nobody has to take any of these. The box mines to your own wallet with none of them. They're conveniences on top, priced later:

managedwe watch it, update it, and tune it, with priority support. Opt-in.
hostingwe run the rig in our space, on our power and cooling. A bigger lift — likely a waitlist before it's real, not day one.
membershipmanaged, plus community and extras. Opt-in.
a poolan optional pool the rigs can point at, with a small fee (1–2%) taken only from what you actually earn — so it's ~nothing while the coin is worth ~nothing. You can mine solo or point anywhere else instead; a pool should never hold most of the network.

Same honest note as above: early mining earns little, and power or hosting can cost more than it makes. These are convenience and a bet on the network — not income.

the wall — why the rig is kept apart

DAARX and L10N have no company behind them — nothing to buy, no one to pressure. That's the whole design, and it doesn't change here. The rig program is a separate thing that sells hardware and help; the protocols stay entity-less on purpose. There is no company formed yet — nothing on this page is a live business, an offering, or a solicitation. When there's a real seller, it will be plainly separate from the coins, and it will never touch how the coins launch.
