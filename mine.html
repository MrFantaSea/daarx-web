Mine it.

When the network's live, anyone can help secure it on a normal computer. No warehouse. Here's how you take part.

The chains use RandomX — mining a regular CPU does well and a datacenter doesn't. Run a node, point a miner at it, and you help hold the network up. Mining DAARX secures all three at once. The node and miner are free and open source, and always will be.

read this first

Participation, not profit. A fair-launch coin starts at zero, on purpose. Early on, the power your rig draws can cost more than it mines. You do this to own in early and help hold the network up, not to make money. No promise of profit.

The coins are never for sale. No premine, no presale, no token sale — mining is the only way in, for everyone. The rig is a separate thing: it's hardware. Buying a box gets you a computer, not coin. There's no company yet, and nothing here is an offering.
