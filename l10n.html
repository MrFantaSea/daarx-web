why a second coin

A coin you hoard and a coin you spend want opposite things. DAARX is scarce by law and never grows. L10N keeps a small, steady trickle of new coins forever — designed to move, not to sit. Same privacy, opposite temperament.

the cash part

Like cash, on your phone: tap, pay, done — and no one watching the chain sees who paid whom, or how much. It's built toward the hardest promise in digital money: working with no internet. No power, no signal, off the grid — cards stop, banks stop. The offline layer launches with the coin if it passes its tests; the coin doesn't wait for it. That's a promise about testing, not a promise about luck.

Its smallest unit is called a tooth.

No signal.still money

Cards stop, banks stop, even Bitcoin stops. L10N is built to keep going — the hardest promise in digital money, so the offline layer ships only once it's passed the testing to earn it.

what's not settled

The exact block cadence and the exact emission numbers get measured, not guessed — the public test network exists to set them from real data before anything is frozen. What we won't do is publish precise numbers today and quietly change them later.

The full whitepaper — the emission math, the offline protocol, the merged-mining mechanics, the limitations — publishes with the test network, late 2026, once its numbers are exact.
Private cash you mine.

L10N starts at zero and comes only from mining — no premine, no presale, no coins for sale. Every DAARX rig secures L10N for free, so the same machine earns you both. You don't buy in, you earn in.
