Break it.

The privacy is new cryptography. New cryptography earns trust one way: people try to break it, in the open, and the attempts get published — the ones that failed and the ones that didn't.

the deal before launch

Anyone can attack the code before it ever holds real value — and there's a bounty on it (below). We write down, in advance, exactly what "a break" would look like — so "we found nothing" means something instead of "we didn't look." Every attempt lands on this log. If a real break is found, the launch waits until it's fixed and re-tested. The date can move; this can't be skipped.

the test network — where you come in

For months before launch, all three chains run in public with coins that are worthless on purpose. Anything goes: fuzz it, forge it, try to spend what isn't yours, try to see what should be hidden. Nothing you can do to the test network costs anyone anything — that's the point of it existing.

1Grab test coins from the faucet and a node from run-a-node (both live when the testnet drops).
2Try to break something real — soundness (make money from nothing, double-spend) or privacy (de-anonymize a payment).
3Report it here (intake below). Reproducible + real → it goes on the log with your name.

the bounty pool + the credit board

There's a $1,000 bounty pool on the whole testnet phase. Break something real and fix it, and you earn a share of the pool — shares are weighted by severity, one award per distinct root cause — plus a seat at the launch party, champagne's on us. A capped pool, not per-bug prizes: this is a fair launch with no war chest, and a small number honestly promised beats a big one that couldn't be paid. Every genuine find — fixed or not — earns permanent public credit on the record that outlives the bug: the person who found the thing everyone else missed. When the whitepapers publish, the log publishes with them. No token, no coins for sale — on either side. The pre-committed break definition above is the bar; official terms post with the intake. When it pays: awards are settled at mainnet launch — there's no guaranteed pay date, because the launch itself is readiness-gated and the date can move (the testing never shrinks to hit one). The credit is immediate and permanent; the cash waits for a real chain. And the log never closes — it keeps running after launch, for as long as the chains do.
