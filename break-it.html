the deal before launch

The project plans to define what a meaningful break looks like before a public testnet and to make launch wait for verified fixes and retesting. No public testnet, vulnerability intake, bounty, award, or publication promise is currently active.

$1,000proposed pool · not funded or offered

This is a draft program concept only. No one can currently earn money, credit, an invitation, or any other award. Official rules and confirmed funding must be published before submissions open.

the test network — where you come in

The future plan is to run public test networks with valueless test coins and explicit scopes for fuzzing, consensus, privacy, networking, wallet, and operational testing. Those networks and their safe-harbor/scope terms are not live.

1Wait for a signed public release, testnet scope, faucet, node package, and disclosure instructions.
2Try to break something real — soundness (make money from nothing, double-spend) or privacy (de-anonymize a payment).
3Use the future private security intake and follow its disclosure rules. Do not send sensitive vulnerability details through the hardware-interest form.

proposed bounty + credit board

A capped $1,000 total pool has been discussed, but it is not funded, governed by official rules, or offered to the public. Eligibility, jurisdictions, age, taxes, sanctions, severity, duplicates, root-cause grouping, fix requirements, safe harbor, disclosure timing, payout source, dispute process, and event terms all remain unresolved.

A public credit board is also a proposal. Publication requires the reporter’s informed consent and a disclosure process that does not expose an unfixed vulnerability or personal information. Until those controls exist, this page creates no entitlement or promise.

No payment date exists. If a program opens, its official rules—not this draft—will control any award, credit, or recognition.
